An exploration of different drivetrain setups

This explanation will try and explain how different drivetrains are layed out and the advantages and disadvantages assosiated with each one.

RWD v.s. FWD v.s. AWD

RWD

RWD or rear wheel drive means that the rear wheels are the ones that a powered by the engine. Rear wheel drive can be done i a few different ways. the most common is a longitudinal orientation. This means that the engine is mounted with the crankshaft paralel to the rear wheels.

FWD

Front Wheel drive means that the front wheels are the ones powerd by the engine. The most common engine orientation in this drive system is a transversly mounted engine, meaning mounted side to side.

More Info

AWD

All whell drive means that all four wheels are powered. These can come in a variety of engine orientations.

More Info

RWD Engine Placment

In a RWD vehicle the engine could be mounted in the front of the car, the middle of the car, or the rear of the car. Companies do this inorder to prioritize different things. A rear engine car is going to have the best traction when accelerating. A mid engine car is the most balanced and will handle the best. A front engine car is the easiest to fix and do any upgrades to.

FWD Engine Placment

There is only one common location that a front wheel drive vehicle has its engine mounted; the front. In a front wheel drive application the engine and transmission will be mounted under the hood. Depending on what country the vehicle is from the engine could be on the passenger side or the driver side.

AWD Engine Placment

In an all wheel drive application the engine could be found in the front, middle or back of the vehicle. It could also be mounted in different directions.
